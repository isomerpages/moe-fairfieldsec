<p>Overview</p>
<p>In Fairfield National Cadet Corps (Land), we aim to develop our cadets’
self-discipline and initiative to help others. We believe strongly in the
‘Cadet Strong’ framework, where all cadets have a resilient mind, fit body
and committed heart.
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>Unit Vision</strong>
<br>A Unit to cultivate cadets to be extraordinary youth leaders who embody
the core values of the Corps.
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>Unit Mission</strong>
<br>Through fun and adventurous military-related activities, we hope
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;To build a strong sense of camaraderie in all our
cadets,
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;To nurture our cadets to be resilient, resourceful,
responsible and respectable,
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;To provide opportunities for our cadets to learn,
grow and to inspire their peers,
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;To develop our cadets’ social awareness and personal
conviction to be a better person and citizen.
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>History</strong>
<br>Fairfield’s NCC Unit started in 1968 as a Girls Unit, which was eventually
closed in 1982. It restarted in 1985 as a Boys Unit, and we have grown
strength to strength since then as an esteemed uniformed group. Our Unit
is especially proud of our Precision Drill Squad (PDS) which has attained
commendable results in HQ NCC competitions thus far. This stands testament
to the unwavering commitment of our NCC (Land) Cadets, Cadet Officers and
Teacher Officers to the continual growth of our Unit and our Corps.
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>Highlights</strong>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>School-based Activities</strong>
<br>Our weekly Friday trainings consist of physical training, sports and games,
foot drills and the teaching of Individual Field Craft to equip our cadets
with basic military knowledge. Our Wednesday trainings are for Secondary
2 and 3 cadets to train to be part of our esteemed Precision Drill Squad.
<br>
</p>
<p>Apart from usual trainings, we organise activities for our cadets to allow
them to experience the outdoors and learn more about Singapore. The main
highlights are camps which are organised by our Secondary 3 Cadet Leaders:
<br>
</p>
<p>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;March Orientation Camp
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;June Camp
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;End-of-Year Camp
<br>
</p>
<p>NCC (Land) Cadets have had the opportunity over the years to participate
in other activities during trainings or camps, including:
<br>
</p>
<p>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Orienteering at Gardens by the Bay
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Night Cycling at East Coast Park
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Dragon Boating and One-Star Kayaking Certification
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Bubble Soccer at Kallang
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Values-in-action Projects
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>NCC Headquarters (NCC HQ) Activities</strong>
<br>
</p>
<p>NCC HQ organises yearly camps such as:
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Camp Forge for Sec 1 Cadets,
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Camp Steel for Sec 2 Cadets,
<br>-&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;Specialist Course for Sec 3 Cadet Leaders.
<br>
</p>
<p>Our Unit also regularly sends cadets to participate in other HQ-led overseas
activities. These opportunities include physical activities such as mountain
trekking in Mt Ophir or Xue Shan (Taiwan), and other military exchange
programmes such as Exercise Wallaby in Australia.</p>
